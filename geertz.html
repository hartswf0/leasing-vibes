The Pattern Latent

Werner Heisenberg points out that technical change alters not only habits of life, but patterns of thought and valuation.

"Whoever uses machines does all his work like a machine. He who does his work like a machine grows a heart like a machine..."

We begin as uncarved geometry—a state of "Simplicity" before the lever, before the grid.

The Machine Heart

The "draw-well" is efficiency. A wooden lever, weighted at the back. It gushes water. But the gardener fears the machine-heart.

To lose simplicity is to become unsure in the strivings of the soul. Industrial logic is a rigid grid, a taxonomy of isolated causes and specific remedies.

"Uncertainty in the strivings of the soul is something which does not agree with honest sense."

Cultural OS

Geertz suggests we are incomplete or unfinished animals who finish ourselves through culture.

Culture is not merely output; it is the generative source code. Without these , man would be a chaos of pointless acts.

"The Cultural OS doesn't just run programs - it creates the user."

Webs of Significance

Man is an animal suspended in webs of significance he himself has spun. To study humanity is to trace the pattern of those webs.

To be human is not just to breathe; it is to control one’s breathing. We begin with the equipment to live a thousand lives, but end having lived only one.

"Culture is the loom on which humanity is woven."
