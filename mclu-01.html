A Kinetic Meditation on

The Medium is the Message

Marshall McLuhan, 1964

SCROLL TO INVOKE MOTION

The Scale of Change: The personal and social consequences of any medium—that is, of any extension of ourselves—result from the new scale that is introduced into our affairs by each extension of ourselves, or by any new technology.

Meditation I

If the "message" is the change of scale, pace, or pattern, what fundamental changes has the smartphone introduced into the scale of your human associations?

The Electric Light: The electric light is pure information. It is a medium without a message, unless it is used to spell out some verbal ad or name. This fact, characteristic of all media, means that the “content” of any medium is always another medium.

Whether the light is being used for brain surgery or night baseball is a matter of indifference. It is the medium that shapes and controls the scale and form of human association and action.

Meditation II

McLuhan argues that the "content" of any medium blinds us to its character. When you use social media, are you watching the "content" or observing how the medium itself reshapes your attention?

The Narcissus Trance: Our conventional response to all media—that it is how they are used that counts—is the numb stance of the technological idiot.

"The 'content' of a medium is like the juicy piece of meat carried by the burglar to distract the watchdog of the mind."

We are too prone to make technological instruments the scapegoats for the sins of those who wield them. To say technology is "neutral" is like saying the smallpox virus is neither good nor bad; it is how it is used that determines its value.

The End of Sequence: The greatest of all reversals occurred with electricity, that ended sequence by making things instant. With instant speed the causes of things began to emerge to awareness again.

Meditation III

In a world of "instant speed," do we lose the ability to understand causality? Does "following" something replace "understanding" it?

The Artist’s Control Tower: The artist is the man in any field, scientific or humanistic, who grasps the implications of his actions and of new knowledge in his own time. He is the man of integral awareness.

In the past century it has come to be generally acknowledged that the artist is always engaged in writing a detailed history of the future because he is the only person aware of the nature of the present.

"I will stand on your eyes, your ears, your nerves, and your brain, and the world will move in any tempo or pattern I choose."

Final Inquiry

If the artist can correct the "sense ratios" before the blow of technology numbs us, what art form today is successfully parrying the violence of our current digital extensions?
